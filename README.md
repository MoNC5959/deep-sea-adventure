# Deep Sea Adventure 🌊

## The Story Behind the Game

I've been a gamer my entire life. From the earliest days of sitting in front of a screen mashing buttons to losing hours to modern titles — games have always been a huge part of who I am.

For just as long, I've had this thought in the back of my mind: *I want to make a game.* Not something complex, just something I could call mine. The problem? Coding has never been my forte. Every time I'd try to pick it up, I'd hit a wall and walk away.

Then came **Claude Code**.

Using Claude Code as my co-pilot, I was finally able to turn that idea into something real. No prior game development experience. No deep knowledge of JavaScript or canvas APIs. Just a vision, a conversation, and a lot of iteration.

## About the Game

Inspired by **Vampire Survivors**, Deep Sea Adventure drops you into the ocean depths as the captain of a submarine — and the deep sea is not happy to see you.

Waves of ocean creatures swarm you from every direction. Dodge, aim, and fire to survive as long as you can.

### Controls

| Input | Action |
|---|---|
| `WASD` / Arrow Keys | Move submarine |
| Mouse | Aim |
| Left Click | Toggle auto-fire (torpedoes) |
| Right Click / `X` | Fire rocket |

### Weapons

- **Torpedo** — Fast, unlimited, fires in the direction you're aiming. Toggle auto-fire with a left click and the sub handles the rest.
- **Rocket** — Slow but devastating. Explodes on contact with an enemy, dealing area damage to everything nearby. You carry 3 rockets, each reloading independently over 8 seconds.

### Enemies

- 🐠 **Clownfish** — Fast and fragile. Easy to kill, worth 10 points.
- 🪼 **Jellyfish** — Drifts erratically. 2 HP, worth 20 points.
- 🐡 **Pufferfish** — Slow but tough. Puffs up and sprouts spikes when hit. 3 HP, worth 30 points.
- 🐟 **Anglerfish** — The real threat. Fast, aggressive, and hard to shake. 2 HP, worth 50 points.

Difficulty ramps up over time — enemies get faster and spawn more frequently the longer you survive.
